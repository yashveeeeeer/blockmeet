Bihar
478%

Average district nightlight intensity in Bihar grew 478% between 2012 and 2024 — roughly 6× the national rate. Bihar dominates the fastest-growing districts list.

Mean radiance: 0.294 (2012) → 1.697 (2024). Growth concentrated in rural districts gaining grid access for the first time.

02

Delhi vs
The Rest

NCT of Delhi glows at 37× the national district average. Central Delhi records mean radiance of 58.5 — over 200× the darkest districts in Arunachal Pradesh.

Delhi 2024 mean: 37.12. National district mean: ~2.35. The ratio has narrowed from ~45× in 2012 as other regions grew faster.

Inequality
Narrowing

Cross-district coefficient of variation: 3.91 in 2012, 2.47 in 2024. A 37% drop. Lagging districts are catching up to urban cores.

CV measures spread relative to mean. Falling CV with a rising mean = broad-based gains. Bottom districts grew faster than top ones.

04

Noida &
Gurgaon

Gautam Buddha Nagar (Noida) doubled nightlight intensity to 14.5. Gurgaon nearly doubled to 12.5. Both outpaced Mumbai over the period.

Mumbai district avg: ~8.2. NCR satellite cities grew faster than legacy metros, driven by IT and services corridor expansion.

The Northeast

Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Andaman & Nicobar — all below 0.40 average in 2024. But zero districts in India got darker over 12 years. Every region gained.

Lowest recorded: Upper Dibang Valley, Arunachal Pradesh at 0.08. Even at that level, 2024 readings are roughly 3× what they were in 2012.
